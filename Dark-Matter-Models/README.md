# Directional Dark Matter Detection

## Overview
This project develops a Python-based simulation for directional dark matter detection, focusing on recoil energy spectra, event-rate modelling, and sensitivity to physical parameters.

## Key Features
- Simulation of recoil energy distributions  
- Directional event-rate modelling  
- Sensitivity analysis to laboratory velocity  

## Approach
The model simulates interactions between dark matter particles and detector nuclei, producing recoil events with measurable energies and directions.

Two velocity configurations were analysed:
- Baseline: [0, 220, 0]  
- Perturbed: [30, 220, 20]  

The resulting distributions were compared to evaluate how changes in system motion affect signal structure.

## Outcome
The project demonstrates how directional information influences detection sensitivity and highlights the importance of modelling assumptions in interpreting signals.
